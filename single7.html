7. DO YOUR TAXIS PROVIDE BABY CAR SEATS?

Yes and no. Car seats are not required in our taxicabs. Still, we have a limited number of car seats that can be reserved on a first come first serve basis. You can either bring your own, or keep the child on your lap and secure yourselves with the normal seat belts. It is legal to not have a car seat, as long as the child is in the lap of a belted adult who is sitting in the back seat.

13. I HAVE TRIED OUT MORE THAN ONE TAXI COMPANY AND RECEIVED POOR TAXI SERVICE BOTH TIMES. WHY?

Dear Valued Taxi Customer, If you are experiencing "poor" taxi service the principal reason it as follows: In recent years we have regularly submitted our statistical reports to the Taxi Division. The reports clearly show that our capacity (number of taxis) and demand (people calling for service) do not match and that there is an excess of taxi cabs in the area. Overall our experience is that we have more cabs than demand. Because of this the bigger companies are not able to grow and provide a better quality of service and invest in better and brighter technology that will be beneficial to everyone in the long run. There are too many one owner operators who either lose their job and open up a taxi business or taxi drivers who drive for the big companies and just end up stealing business and opening up their own taxi company. This not only has brought the cost of taxis down, but has greatly affected the taxi service we are able to provide. We have approached all of the regulators repeatedly in the area. The . We try to get a cab for everyone who wants one, but the numbers make that difficult: we have about 25 vehicles on the streets. At peak times most of those cabs are either occupied, en route to orders, or changing shifts. If the public complains enough to the authorities, the City may release sufficient new licenses to meet the demand.
